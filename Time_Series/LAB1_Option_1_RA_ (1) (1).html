data=pd.read_csv("trends-gss.csv")

I want to analyze the trend in the number of children considered ideal over time and if variables like the number of years of education affected this number (chldidl)

I am going to pick the ideal number of children, year, sex, age and education ( number of years) as my variables of interest.

I picked the 'ideal number of children' from the 'Attitudes towards family and children section' to understand what people think is the ideal number of children to have and how it has changed over time. I picked 'age' and 'sex' as variables to see if there is any difference in what males and females perceive as the ideal number of children to have and to see how people of different ages feel about the ideal number of children for them. I picked education because I wanted to see if more years of studying had any effect on the ideal number of children considered.

This does make sense because people with higher levels of education are more aware obout family planning and are more mindful of the resources required to raise a large number of children. The model has an r-squared value of 0.007 which is not very great but means that 0.7% of the variation in the opinion on the avergae ideal number of children can be explained by the number of years of education a person has had.
